Hong Kong food

The Hong Kong Food Culture Department is where the South Guangdong culture meets the British culture and develops a set of Cantonese and Western food habits. Japanese, Korean, Taiwanese, Vietnamese, French, Thai, and Indian dishes are found in Hong Kong, making them a “food paradise”.

In the development of Hong Kong culture, diet is important and interesting. In the past, the big-name brand filed with the street food stalls such as Da Lai,the old-fashioned restaurant and the various kinds of authentic soy sauce and cakes with the characteristics of the food, have become a collective memory of Hong Kong people, and continue to be handed down.

Over the past 100 years, the rapid development of the Hong Kong economy and the Hong Kong society has brought new flavors to Hong Kong people's food culture. Today, the diet has only been filled with the past, and it has developed into a beautiful pursuit of color and fragrance. The restaurant is also simple and simple, and even the decoration is gorgeous. The dishes are tasted by authentic tastes. It is enough to reflect the changing attitude of Hong Kong people's diet and quality of life.
